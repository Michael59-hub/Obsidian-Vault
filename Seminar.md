Difference b/w transporter and pharmacokinetic hypothesis: Transporter focuses more on drug transporters while pharmacokinetic encompasses that and also focuses on metabolism.

Difference b/w target and gene variation hypothesis: Target focuses only on alterations in structure/function of drug targets while gene variant encompasses drug targets, ion channels and CYP enzymes

## SCN1A Gene
- This codes for the voltage gated sodium ion channels neuronal type 1, alpha subunit. NAv 1.1
- The sodium channel it codes for is present in inhibitory cells so blocking these sodium channels actually increases excitation

## VNS and RNS
- RNS(Responsive NeuroStimulation) fires electric pulses into the brain during an epileptic emergency which are fine tuned to activate the inhibitory mechanism, make all the neurons enter refractory period at the same time, and desynchronize the neuronal firing.
- VNS (Vagus Nerve Stimulation) is  a long term solution that send pulses through the vagus nerve into the brain which causes release of norepinephrine and serotonin and makes the brain less susceptible to epileptic episodes.
