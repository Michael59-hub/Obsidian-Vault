The diagnosis of a Major Depression Episode (MDE) requires five or more symptoms to be present within a 2-week period. The symptoms are:
1. Depressed mood

2. Diminished interest or pleasure in all or most activities (anhedonia)

3. Unintentional significant weight loss or gain

4. Insomnia or hypersomnia

5. Agitation or psychomotor retardation

6. Fatigue or loss of energy

7. Diminished ability to concentrate

8. Recurrent thoughts of death

## Pathophysiology of Depression
### 1. Monoamine theory of depression
This theory states that depression is as a result of a deficiency in the amount or function of monoamines in the cortical and limbic pathways.
[[Reserpine]] which is used in clinical settings depletes dopamine and has been linked to depression.
[^1]Similarly, depressed patients who respond to serotonergic antidepressants such as [[Fluoxetine]] often rapidly suffer relapse when given diets free of tryptophan, a precursor of serotonin synthesis

### 2. Neurotrophic Hypothesis
This theory suggests that depression is linked or caused by a loss of neurotrophic support with [[BDNF(Brain derived Neurotrophic Factor)]] being central.
The evidence for this theory suggests that depression is associated with the loss of neurotrophic support and that effective antidepressant therapies increase neurogenesis and synaptic connectivity in cortical areas such as the hippocampus.
Animal and human models have shown that stress and pain are associated with a drop in BDNF levels and this leads to atrophy of the cortical areas such as the hippocampus, medial frontal cortex etc.
Another point of evidence is that fact that direct infusion of BDNF into the cortical areas produces antidepressant like effects. Also, all known classes of antidepressants are associated with an increase in BDNF levels with chronic administration.
Other interventions used to treat depression such as Electroconvulsive therapy also lead to an increase in BDNF levels.
Additionally though, stress doesn't always lead to a drop in BDNF levels. Sometimes it increases it. I guess that's why the theory can't be accepted.
