## MOA 
It has two MOAs: adenosine receptor antagonism and [[Phosphodiesterase (PDE)]] inhibition. 
PDE inhibition causes an increase in cAMP levels which leads to:
- **Bronchial smooth muscle relaxation** → bronchodilation
    
- **Cardiac stimulation** → ↑ heart rate & contractility
    
- **Reduced mediator release** from mast cells (↓ histamine, leukotrienes).

Adenosine normally causes:
- CNS depression
	
- Bronchoconstriction
	
- Vasodilation
	
- ↓ heart rate
	

Blocking adenosine causes:
- CNS stimulation (alertness, insomnia, tremors)
    
- Respiratory center stimulation
    
- Bronchodilation
    
- Tachycardia
