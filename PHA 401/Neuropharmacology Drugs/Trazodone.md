## Pharmacokinetics
- Its primary metabolite, m-chlorphenylpiperazine (m-cpp), is a potent 5-HT 2 antagonist.

- Extensive metabolism

- Extensively bound to protein and have limited bioavailability because of extensive metabolism.

- Short half-lives generally require split dosing

[[5 HT-2 Antagonists]]

## Notes
- The most common use of trazodone in current practice is as an unlabeled hypnotic, since it is highly sedating and not associated with tolerance or dependence
