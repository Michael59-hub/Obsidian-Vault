These are also known as ANALEPTICS. They increase the activity of the brain and spinal cord resulting in increased alertness, reduced fatigue, enhanced attention, improved mood and increased motor and cognitive ability.

They are used to treat [[Narcolepsy]], depression, ADHD etc.

## Convulsant Stimulants
Convulsant stimulants are CNS stimulants that induce seizures (convulsions) by causing excessive neuronal excitation, reducing inhibitory neurotransmission (especially GABA & glycine), or enhancing excitatory neurotransmitters such as glutamate. They aren't used therapeutically; just for experiments and toxicological inquiry. Examples include: [[Strychnine]], [[Picrotoxin]], [[Bicuculine]],[[Nikethemide]], [[Amphetamine]], [[Cocaine]]

### Pharmacological / Toxicological Effects Of Convulsant Stimulants

- Central Nervous System: Anxiety, Tremors → Clonus, Tonic–clonic convulsions, Status epilepticus and Coma in severe cases.

- Neuromuscular Effects: Muscle rigidity, Hyper-reflexia and Spasms

- Cardiovascular: Tachycardia, Hypertension and Arrhythmias (especially cocaine, theophylline)

- Respiratory Effects: Increased respiratory rate (early) and Respiratory depression/failure (late, due to prolonged seizures)

- Metabolic: Hyperthermia, Lactic acidosis and Rhabdomyolysis (prolonged convulsions).

## Management of Convulsant Stimulant Poisoning
- Enhance GABA-ergic activity through the use of [[Benzodiazepines]] and [[Barbiturates]]
- Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) for isoniazid-induced seizures
