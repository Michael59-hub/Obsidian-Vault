Extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) are drug-induced movement disorders, commonly caused by antipsychotic medications and dopamine-blocking agents, affecting motor control. Key symptoms include acute dystonia (painful muscle spasms), akathisia (restlessness), parkinsonism (tremors, stiffness), and tardive dyskinesia (involuntary, jerky movements). They occur when dopamine levels are disrupted in the basal ganglia, requiring, as described in [ScienceDirect.com](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1555415507002966). 

**Key Aspects of Extrapyramidal Symptoms:**

- **Common Causes:** Antipsychotics (especially high-potency/first-generation), antiemetics, and some antidepressants.
- **Key Symptoms:**

    - **[Akathisia](https://www.google.com/search?q=Akathisia&rlz=1C1GCEA_en-GBNG1194NG1194&oq=what+are+extrapyramidal+&gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUqDQgAEAAYkQIYgAQYigUyDQgAEAAYkQIYgAQYigUyBwgBEAAYgAQyBggCEEUYOTIHCAMQABiABDIHCAQQABiABDIHCAUQABiABDIHCAYQABiABDIHCAcQABiABDIHCAgQABiABDIHCAkQABiABNIBCDYwMzhqMGo5qAIGsAIB8QUuosGRdVSi2Q&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8&mstk=AUtExfBmOBrl9L_DxIOlsas0Us4tmwufvGcIy4MGnkrP41H4zzwveKoy_1daVa3YEYxIiNjqsj3ims0YYx01ElIgXPHh2ebmQbCn8VnLJNbKqath2nir5FpPnMahUZ_PURkhTsh_vyOoR6r0izrNWRb3g4RU9456mU5DVdMivzNLpxJ5h_k&csui=3&ved=2ahUKEwiNierZo7aSAxWcJBAIHSCsH28QgK4QegQIBBAF):** Intense motor restlessness, appearing as an inability to sit still, constant pacing, or fidgeting.
    - **[Pseudoparkinsonism](https://www.google.com/search?q=Pseudoparkinsonism&rlz=1C1GCEA_en-GBNG1194NG1194&oq=what+are+extrapyramidal+&gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUqDQgAEAAYkQIYgAQYigUyDQgAEAAYkQIYgAQYigUyBwgBEAAYgAQyBggCEEUYOTIHCAMQABiABDIHCAQQABiABDIHCAUQABiABDIHCAYQABiABDIHCAcQABiABDIHCAgQABiABDIHCAkQABiABNIBCDYwMzhqMGo5qAIGsAIB8QUuosGRdVSi2Q&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8&mstk=AUtExfBmOBrl9L_DxIOlsas0Us4tmwufvGcIy4MGnkrP41H4zzwveKoy_1daVa3YEYxIiNjqsj3ims0YYx01ElIgXPHh2ebmQbCn8VnLJNbKqath2nir5FpPnMahUZ_PURkhTsh_vyOoR6r0izrNWRb3g4RU9456mU5DVdMivzNLpxJ5h_k&csui=3&ved=2ahUKEwiNierZo7aSAxWcJBAIHSCsH28QgK4QegQIBBAH):** Tremor, rigidity, slowness of movement (bradykinesia), and a shuffling gait. Commonly caused by ==haloperidol==
    - **[Tardive Dyskinesia (TD)](https://www.google.com/search?q=Tardive+Dyskinesia+%28TD%29&rlz=1C1GCEA_en-GBNG1194NG1194&oq=what+are+extrapyramidal+&gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUqDQgAEAAYkQIYgAQYigUyDQgAEAAYkQIYgAQYigUyBwgBEAAYgAQyBggCEEUYOTIHCAMQABiABDIHCAQQABiABDIHCAUQABiABDIHCAYQABiABDIHCAcQABiABDIHCAgQABiABDIHCAkQABiABNIBCDYwMzhqMGo5qAIGsAIB8QUuosGRdVSi2Q&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8&mstk=AUtExfBmOBrl9L_DxIOlsas0Us4tmwufvGcIy4MGnkrP41H4zzwveKoy_1daVa3YEYxIiNjqsj3ims0YYx01ElIgXPHh2ebmQbCn8VnLJNbKqath2nir5FpPnMahUZ_PURkhTsh_vyOoR6r0izrNWRb3g4RU9456mU5DVdMivzNLpxJ5h_k&csui=3&ved=2ahUKEwiNierZo7aSAxWcJBAIHSCsH28QgK4QegQIBBAJ):** Long-term, sometimes irreversible, involuntary movements, often affecting the face, such as tongue thrusting, lip-smacking, or jaw clenching.
