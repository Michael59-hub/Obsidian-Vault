### **Drug class**

- Oral hypoglycaemic agents
    
- **Insulin secretagogues**
    

---

### **Chemical nature**

- Sulfonamide derivatives
    
- Weak acids
    
- Highly plasma protein–bound
    

---

### **Classification**

**First generation**

- Tolbutamide
    
- Chlorpropamide
    
- Tolazamide
    

**Second generation** (more potent, commonly used)

- Glibenclamide (glyburide)
    
- Glipizide
    
- Gliclazide
    
- Glimepiride
    

---

### **Mechanism of action (key mechanism)**

Sulfonylureas **stimulate insulin release from pancreatic β-cells**.

1. Bind to **SUR1 subunit** of ATP-sensitive K⁺ channels on β-cell membrane
    
2. **Closure of K⁺ channels**
    
3. Membrane depolarization
    
4. Opening of voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels
    
5. Ca²⁺ influx → **insulin exocytosis**
    

➡️ Require **functional β-cells**

---

### **Pharmacological effects**

- ↑ Insulin secretion (basal and stimulated)
    
- ↓ Fasting and post-prandial blood glucose
    
- ↓ HbA1c (≈ 1–2%)
    

---

### **Pharmacokinetics**

**Absorption**

- Well absorbed orally
    

**Distribution**

- Extensive plasma protein binding (90–99%)
    

**Metabolism**

- Hepatic metabolism (CYP450 enzymes)
    

**Excretion**

- Renal ± biliary excretion
    
- Some active metabolites (e.g. glibenclamide)
    

---

### **Therapeutic uses**

- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus**
    
    - When diet/exercise ± metformin are inadequate
        
- Not effective in type 1 DM
    

---

### **Adverse effects**

**Common**

- **Hypoglycaemia** (major limitation)
    
- Weight gain
    

**Others**

- Nausea
    
- Skin rashes
    
- Photosensitivity
    

**Rare**

- Cholestatic jaundice
    
- Hematological effects (leukopenia, thrombocytopenia)
    
- Disulfiram-like reaction (chlorpropamide)
    

---

### **Contraindications**

- **Type 1 diabetes mellitus**
    
- Pregnancy and lactation
    
- Severe hepatic or renal disease
    
- Elderly patients (higher hypoglycaemia risk)
    

---

### **Drug interactions**

**Increase hypoglycaemic effect**

- Alcohol
    
- NSAIDs
    
- Sulfonamides
    
- Warfarin
    
- MAO inhibitors
    

**Decrease effect**

- Thiazide diuretics
    
- Corticosteroids
    
- Phenytoin
    
- Oral contraceptives
    

---

### **Advantages**

- Effective and inexpensive
    
- Rapid glucose-lowering effect
    

---

### **Disadvantages**

- Hypoglycaemia
    
- Weight gain
    
- Secondary failure with long-term use
    

---

### **Differences between generations**

- Second-generation drugs are:
    
    - More potent
        
    - Longer duration
        
    - Lower doses required
        
    - Less adverse effects (relatively)
