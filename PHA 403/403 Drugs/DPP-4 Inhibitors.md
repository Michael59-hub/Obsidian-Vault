### **Drug class**

- Oral antidiabetic drugs
    
- **Incretin enhancers**
    

---

### **Drugs (–gliptins)**

- Sitagliptin
    
- Vildagliptin
    
- Saxagliptin
    
- Linagliptin
    
- Alogliptin
    

---

### **Mechanism of action**

DPP-4 inhibitors **prevent the breakdown of [[Incretin]] hormones**.

1. Inhibit **dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4)** enzyme
    
2. ↑ Endogenous **GLP-1 and GIP** levels
    
3. **Glucose-dependent** effects:
    
    - ↑ Insulin secretion from β-cells
        
    - ↓ Glucagon release from α-cells
        

➡️ Result: ↓ fasting and post-prandial glucose  
➡️ **No insulin release at low glucose levels**

---

### **Pharmacological effects**

- Moderate reduction in blood glucose
    
- ↓ HbA1c ≈ **0.5–0.8%**
    
- Improve post-prandial glucose control
    
- **Weight neutral**
    
- Minimal risk of hypoglycaemia (monotherapy)
    

---

### **Pharmacokinetics (general)**

**Absorption**

- Well absorbed orally
    

**Distribution**

- Low–moderate plasma protein binding
    

**Metabolism**

- Mostly minimal hepatic metabolism
    
- Saxagliptin → active metabolite (CYP3A4/5)
    

**Excretion**

- Mainly **renal** (except **linagliptin → biliary/fecal**)
    

---

### **Therapeutic uses**

- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus**
    
    - Monotherapy (when metformin not tolerated)
        
    - Add-on therapy with metformin, sulfonylureas, or insulin
        

---

### **Adverse effects**

**Common**

- Nasopharyngitis
    
- Headache
    
- Upper respiratory tract infections
    

**Less common**

- Gastrointestinal upset
    
- Skin reactions
    

**Rare but important**

- **Acute pancreatitis**
    
- Severe joint pain
    
- Hypersensitivity reactions (angioedema, SJS – very rare)
    

---

### **Contraindications / precautions**

- History of pancreatitis (relative)
    
- Renal impairment → dose adjustment required (except linagliptin)
    

---

### **Drug interactions**

- Saxagliptin + strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g. ketoconazole) → ↑ drug levels
    
- Low potential for significant drug interactions overall
    

---

### **Advantages**

- Oral administration
    
- **Low hypoglycaemia risk**
    
- Weight neutral
    
- Well tolerated in elderly patients
    

---

### **Disadvantages**

- Modest glucose-lowering efficacy
    
- Cost
    
- Possible pancreatitis risk
    

---

### **Comparison pearl**

- **DPP-4 inhibitors** → increase **endogenous** incretins
    
- **GLP-1 agonists** → give **exogenous** incretin effect
