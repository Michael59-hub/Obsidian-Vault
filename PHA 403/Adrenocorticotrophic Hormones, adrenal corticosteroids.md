The adrenal gland is made up of 2 parts: the cortex(80%) and the medulla(20%).
The cortex consists of 3 parts: adrenal glomerulosa(mineralocorticoids), fasciculata(
glucocorticoids) and reticularis(androgens). All corticosteroids are synthesized from steroid cholesterol.
The medulla produces adrenaline and norepinephrine.


## Mineralocorticoids
These affect minerals of the extracellular fluids
- Aldosterone: Very potent and account for 90% of all glucocorticoid activity
- Desoxycorticosterone: 1/30 as potent but very small amount secreted
- 9a-fluococortisol: Synthetic, slightly more potent

## Glucocorticoids
These affect blood glucose concentration, protein and fat metabolism.
- Cortisol: Very potent and accounts for 95% of all glucocorticoid activity
- Corticosterone: Less potent than cortisol. Accounts for 4% of all glucocorticoid activity
- Cortisone: Synthetic, almost as potent
- Prednisone: 4x as potent
- Methylprednisone: 5x as potent
- Dexamethasone: 30x as potent

## PK of Adrenocortical Hormones
- 90-95% of cortisol binds to plasma proteins specifically a globulin called ==cortisol-binding globulin== or ==transcorin== and to a lesser extent, albumin.
- For aldosterone, the plasma protein binding is 60% which makes for a short half life of 20 minutes.
- Adrenal steroids are metabolised mainly in the liver and are conjugated mainly to glucuronic acid and sulphates. Then excreted 75% in urine and 25% in bile/feaces


## Effect of Glucocorticoids
1. Promote gluconeogenesis
2. Promote lipid metabolism
3. Produce immunosuppression
4. Increases ionotropic and chronotropic effect. They also increase contraction in mid-sized arteries.

## Therapeutic Uses of Glucocorticoids

1. Chronic bronchitis.
2. Emphysema.
3. Sarcoidosis.
4. Ulcerative colitis.
5. Crohn’s disease.
6. SLE
7. cerebral edema.
8. Skin diseases.
