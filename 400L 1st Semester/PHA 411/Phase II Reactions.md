## SULT Isoforms
### 1. SULT1A1
- Also known as thermostable phenol sulfotransferase
- Broadest substrate specificity
- Inhibited by curcumin, NSAIDs, quercetin and some teas&juices

### 2. SULT1A3/4
- Inhibited by some teas and juices
### 3. SULT1B1
- Metabolisers of thyroid hormones
- Most expressed SULT in the GIT
- Directly affects bioavailability of some drugs
### 4. SULT1E1
- AKA estrogen sulfotransferase
- Sulfonates estrogens and iodothyronines
- Most abundant in the lungs
- Inhibited by NSAIDs
### 5. SULT2A1
- Widely expressed in human tissues
- Second most abundant in the liver after 1A1
- Sulfonates bile acids and steroids

## Glucuronic Acid Conjugation
- Before attachment it is converted to an active form/moiety called uridine diphosphate glucuronic acid
- Catalysed by Uridine Diphosphate Glucuronosyltransferase(UGT)
- UGT is present in the smoot ER of liver cells
- There have been 4 UGT families found in humans: UGT 1,2,3 and 8
- UGT2B7 is the major enzyme responsible for the glucuronidation of opioids
- Hecogenin (steroidal saponin) is responsible for inhibition of UGT1A4, and flucoconazole inhibits UGT2B7 activity
- UGT inducers include rifampicin

## Glutathione Conjugation
- Catalysed by glutathione-S-transferases within the cytosol
- GST is inhibited by dopamine, alpha-methyldopa and 5-S-glutathionyldopamine
- Ginkgo biloba extracts induce GST-P1

## Acetylation 
- Takes place in drugs which contains amino groups, hydrazine or hydrazine linkage in their structures e.g. sulfonamides and phenelzine
- Catalysed by acetyltransferase
- It does not increase hydrophilicity of drugs. It terminates the activity of drugs
## Methylation
- Mostly results in activation of compounds
- Catalysed by methyl transferases
