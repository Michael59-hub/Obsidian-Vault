Drug interaction is  measurable modification in magnitude or duration of the action of a drug by a prior or concomitant administration of another substance. Most times the modification is quantitative while other times it is qualitative(different response entirely).

## Index Drug
This is the drug which has its effects modified by the ==Interacting Drug==. When the activities of both drugs are modified then they are both index and interacting drugs.

## Types of Drug Interactions
1. Pharmacokinetic: ADME
2. Pharmacodynamic: Additive, synergistic(summation), (potentiating), or antagonistic effects

Common drug-drug interactions include additive, synergism, potentiation, antidote, complex, displacement(from plasma proteins), inhibition, induction, urinary excretion

## Clinically Useful Drug Interactions
1. Antibiotics + Analgesics for treating painful infective conditions
2. Adrenaline + Lidocaine for anaesthesia
3. ACE Inhibitors + Diuretics for hypertension
4. Sulfamethoxazole + Trimethoprim for bacterial infections
5. Furosemide + Amiloride to prevent hypokalemia
6. Phenobarbitone in DDT intoxication

## Pharmacokinetic Drug Interactions
### Absorption
- Ketoconazole absorption being reduced by H2 blockers and PPIs because they reduce the production of stomach acid which helps with the dissolution of ketoconazole.
- Phenytoin absorption being decreased by sucralfate blue binding in the GI lumen
- Contraceptive absorption being decreased by antibiotics
### Distribution
- Quinidine reducing binding of digoxin to plasma proteins as well as the renal and billary clearance(by inhibiting the efflux transporter p-glycoprotein) thus increasing efficacy/toxicity

### Metabolism
- Enzyme induction is increased synthesis or activity of an enzyme. Common inducers: carbamazepine, phenytoin, rifampicin, phenobarbital, alcohol and smoking
- Enzyme inhibition is the reduction in enzyme activity due to antagonism. Common inhibitors: grape fruit juice, cimetidine, ketoconazole, erythromycin and chloramphenicol
### Excretion
- Caffeine increases blood flow to the kidneys
- Excretion of acidic drugs will increase in alkaline urine and vice versa

## Herb-Drug Interaction
- St John's Wort affects the clearance of drugs such as cyclosporin. It also induces CYP3A4 and P-glycoprotein.

## Drug-Food Interactions
- Eating char boiled food decreases warfarin activity while eating cooked onions may increase its activity
- Green leafy vegetables also decrease warfarin effect
- Propranolol + protein food increases propranolol levels in serum
- Absorption of ACE inhibitors is increased on an empty stomach
- Grape fruit juice increases the bioavailability of felodipine
- Intestinal absorption of celiprolol is reduced in the presence of orange juice
- Milk products inhibits the absorption of antibiotics 
- Azithromycin absorption is decreased when taken with food
- Tetracycline should be taken 1 hour before or 2 hours after meals. Also shouldn't be taken with milk products
- Acetaminophen should be taken on an empty stomach for rapid relief
- NSAIDs should be taken with food or milk
