A biological membrane or biomembrane is a selectively permeable membrane that separates the interior of a cell from the external environment

## Membrane Structure and Function
- Phospholipid Bilayer: A biological membrane or biomembrane is a selectively permeable membrane that separates the interior of a cell from the external environment. It creates a semi-permeable membrane
- Proteins: Receptors, enzymes, transporters, and ion channels. Integral proteins span the membrane, while peripheral proteins are attached to the surface
- Cholesterol: Regulates membrane fluidity and structure, influencing drug interactions
## Pharmacological Processes
1. Passive Diffusion
2. Active Transport
3. Endocytosis
## Drug-Membrane Interactions
1. Drug Absorption: Factors influencing absorption include solubility, permeability, and transport mechanisms.
2. Drug Distribution: Membrane permeability and transport mechanisms
3. Drug Excretion: : Membranes in kidneys and liver influence drug elimination through filtration, secretion, and reabsorption.
## Membrane Related Drug Targets
1. Receptors
2. Enzymes
3. Ion channels
4. Transporters
## Factors Influencing Drug-Membrane Interactions
1. Liphophilicity
2. Charge
3. Molecular size
4. pH

## Pharmacological Significance
Understanding membrane pharmacology is crucial for drug development and therapy, as it influences drug efficacy, toxicity, and distribution. By targeting specific membrane components, drugs can exert therapeutic effects or exhibit adverse reactions.
