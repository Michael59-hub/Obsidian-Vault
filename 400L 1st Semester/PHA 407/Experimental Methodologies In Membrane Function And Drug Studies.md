## Common Model Systems
### 1. Vesicles/Liposomes
- Spherical lipid bilayers, useful for ==encapsulation and permeability studies==.
- They are prepared by extrusion through defined pores or sonication of lipid dispersions
- 
### 2. Langmuir Monolayers
- Single lipid layers at the air–water interface, useful for studying ==lipid packing and drug insertion==.
### 3. Solid-supported Bilayer
- Lipid bilayers on solid substrates(mica, silicon, gold electrodes), suitable for ==surface-sensitive techniques==.
### 4. Tethered Bilayer Lipid Membranes
- Bilayers **anchored to a surface**, providing ==stability and controlled spacing for studies==.
### Advantages
- Well-characterized systems with known properties.
- Enable systematic investigation of membrane-related processes.
- Reduce complexity compared to natural membranes.
### Limitations
- No single model perfectly mimics all properties of a natural membrane.
- Findings must be correlated and validated with natural systems to ensure biological relevance.
## Notes
- The membrane potential of the cell at which there is no net movement for a given ion is called the **equilibrium potential** for that ion

# Channelopathies
These are diseases that develop as a result of defects in ion channels caused by either genetic or acquired factors. Mutations in genes coding ion channels are the most common cause 
## Channelopathies in the Nervous System
### Nervous System & Muscle Disorders

- **Myotonia Congenita**: Muscle stiffness caused by mutations in the $ClC-1$ chloride channel ($CLCN1$ gene).
    
- **Hyperkalemic Periodic Paralysis**: Gain-of-function mutations in $SCN4A$ ($Na^+$ channel) leading to muscle weakness and hyperkalemia.
    
- **Dravet Syndrome**: Severe epilepsy resulting from mutations in $SCN1A$ ($Na^+$ channel) or $GABRG2$ ($GABA$ receptor).
    
- **Andersen-Tawil Syndrome**: Periodic paralysis and cardiac arrhythmias caused by mutations in the $Kir2.1$ potassium channel.
    

### Cardiovascular System

- **Long QT Syndrome (LQTS)**: Characterized by prolonged ventricular repolarization, increasing the risk of sudden death.
    
    - **Loss-of-function** in $K^+$ channels ($KCNQ1$, etc.).
        
    - **Gain-of-function** in $Na^+$ or $Ca^{2+}$ channels ($SCN5A$, $CACNA1C$).
        

### Endocrine & Bone Disorders

- **Neonatal Diabetes Mellitus**: Caused by **gain-of-function** mutations in $K_{ATP}$ channel subunits ($Kir6.2$ or $SUR1$), which keep channels open and reduce insulin secretion.

- **Hyperinsulinemic Hypoglycemia**: Caused by **loss-of-function** mutations in the same $K_{ATP}$ genes.
    
- **Osteopetrosis**: Increased bone mass due to impaired resorption; caused by mutations in the $ClC-7$ chloride channel.
