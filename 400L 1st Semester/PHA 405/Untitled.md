## Designing a Rational Dose Regimen
Notes: In irreversible antagonism, the duration of action is dependent on the rate of turnover of receptor molecules. It is independent on its own rate of elimination.

## Upregulation of Receptors
This is the increased number or responsiveness of receptors
- Could result from disuse of a receptor effector system,
- Prolonged use of antagonists
- Examples include:
### Mechanisms of Upregulation
1. Increased receptor synthesis
2. Decreased receptor degradation
3. Reduced receptor internalisation
4. Increased receptor sensitivity
## Downregulation of Receptors
This is the decreased responsiveness of receptors
- Could result from overexposure of a receptor effector system to agonist
- Also a decrease in number of functional receptors like in Myasthenia gravis 
### Mechanisms of Downregulation
1. Reduced receptor synthesis
2. Increased  receptor degradation
3. Increased receptor internalisation
4. Desensitisation
