### **Drug class**

- **Biguanide**
    
- Oral antihyperglycaemic agent
    

---

### **Chemical nature**

- Synthetic **biguanide** derivative
    
- Highly **hydrophilic**, exists as a cation at physiological pH
    

---

### **Mechanism of action (core points)**

Metformin lowers blood glucose **without stimulating insulin release**.

1. **↓ Hepatic gluconeogenesis** (primary action)
    
    - Activates **AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK)**
        
    - Inhibits mitochondrial respiratory chain complex I
        
    - ↓ ATP → ↑ AMP → suppresses gluconeogenic enzymes
        
2. **↑ Peripheral insulin sensitivity**
    
    - Enhances glucose uptake in skeletal muscle and adipose tissue (↑ GLUT-4 activity)
        
3. **↓ Intestinal glucose absorption**
    
    - Delays and reduces glucose uptake from the gut
        

➡️ Net effect: **↓ fasting and post-prandial blood glucose**

---

### **Pharmacological effects**

- ↓ Fasting plasma glucose
    
- ↓ Post-prandial glucose
    
- ↓ HbA1c (≈ 1–1.5%)
    
- **No hypoglycaemia when used alone**
    
- **Weight neutral or mild weight loss**
    
- ↓ Serum triglycerides and LDL cholesterol
    

---

### **Pharmacokinetics**

**Absorption**

- Absorbed from the small intestine
    
- Bioavailability ≈ **50–60%**
    

**Distribution**

- Negligible plasma protein binding
    
- Accumulates in liver, kidneys, and intestinal wall
    

**Metabolism**

- **Not metabolized** (important exam point)
    

**Excretion**

- Excreted **unchanged by kidneys** via tubular secretion
    
- Half-life: ~ **6 hours**
    

---

### **Therapeutic uses**

- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus** (first-line drug)
    
- Prediabetes (impaired glucose tolerance)
    
- **Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)**
    
- Obese patients with insulin resistance
    
- Prevention of diabetes in high-risk patients
    

---

### **Adverse effects**

**Common**

- Gastrointestinal upset:
    
    - Nausea
        
    - Diarrhoea
        
    - Abdominal discomfort
        
    - Metallic taste
        

**Long-term**

- **Vitamin B12 deficiency**
    

**Rare but serious**

- **Lactic acidosis** (life-threatening)
    

---

### **Contraindications**

- **Renal impairment** (eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73 m²)
    
- Severe hepatic disease
    
- Congestive heart failure with hypoxia
    
- Alcoholism
    
- Conditions predisposing to hypoxia (sepsis, shock)
    

---

### **Drug interactions**

- Alcohol → ↑ risk of lactic acidosis
    
- Iodinated contrast media → risk of acute kidney injury (withhold metformin temporarily)
    

---

### **Advantages**

- No hypoglycaemia
    
- Weight neutral / weight loss
    
- Cardiovascular benefit
    
- Low cost
    

---

### **Disadvantages**

- GI intolerance
    
- Risk of lactic acidosis in renal failure
    
- B12 deficiency with long-term use
