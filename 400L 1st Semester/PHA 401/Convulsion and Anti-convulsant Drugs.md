Convulsion refers to sudden, involuntary, violent, and irregular contractions of groups of skeletal muscles, usually resulting from abnormal, excessive, synchronous neuronal discharges in the brain.

Convulsion is commonly associated with epilepsy, but can also arise from fever, toxins, metabolic disturbances, infections, head injuries, or drug withdrawal.

The primary neurophysiological event in convulsion is hyperexcitability and hypersynchronization of neurons in specific brain regions (e.g., hippocampus, cortex).


## Epilepsy vs Seizure vs Convulsion
Epilepsy: A chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent, unprovoked seizures resulting from persistent cerebral hyperexcitability.

Seizure: **Refers** to abnormal electrical activity in the brain which can **manifest** as changes in behavior, consciousness, or motor function.

Convulsion: refers to sudden, involuntary, violent, and irregular contractions of groups of skeletal muscles, usually resulting from abnormal, excessive, synchronous neuronal discharges in the brain.

N.B: Seizure refers to the abnormal electrical activity while convulsion is a physical manifestation of the abnormal electrical activity where there is sudden, involuntary and irregular contractions of groups of skeletal muscles.

## Classification of Seizures/Epilepsy
## 1. Focal Seizures
They originate from a specific localised area of one cerebral hemisphere. They are further divided into:
- Focal aware seizures (simple partial)
- Focal Impaired Awareness Seizures (Complex Partial)
- Focal to Bilateral Tonic-Clonic Seizures
- Focal Motor Onset
- Focal Non-Motor Onset
