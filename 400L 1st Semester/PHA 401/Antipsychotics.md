## Schizophrenia
This is a clinicals syndrome of variable but profoundly disruptive psychopathology characterised by a disintegration of thought and emotional responsiveness with manifestations of auditory hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech with significant social and/or occupational dysfunction.


## Symptoms
The symptoms are in two parts: Acute syndrome which has positive symptoms and then chronic syndrome which has negative symptoms.
### Acute
•Delusions
•Hallucinations
•Inference with thought
• Incongruity of affect
• Precipitated by too much social stimulation

### Chronic
•Social withdrawal;
•Apathy
•Poverty of speech
•Blunting affect
•Formal thought disorder
•Slowness
•Lack of drive and initiatives
• Age disorientation
•Precipitated by too little social stimulation


## Risk factors for Conversion to Psychosis
1. Genetic predisposition
2. Substance abuse
3. Young age

N.B: Candidate genes are genes suspected to be involved in the pathophysiology of psychosis. E.g. Human proline dehydrogenase

## Theories and Hypothesis
## 1. Dopamine hypothesis of Schizophrenia

### Dopaminergic Pathways in Schizophrenia
1. Mesolimbic: Made of projections from the ventral tegmental area to the fore brain. Plays a key role in **motivation, emotion, reward** and +ve symptoms of schizophrenia. This pathway is responsible for the **positive symptoms**.
2. Mesocortical: Made of projections from the ventral tegmental area to the prefrontal cortex. Plays key roles in cognition/executive function, emotions. This pathway is responsible for the **negative symptoms**.
3. Nigrostriatal: Contains 80% of the brain's dopamine. This pathway is involved in **motor planning**, dopaminergic neurons stimulate purposeful movement. D2 antagonism induces [[Extrapyramidal Symptoms]] and the case of first generation antipsychotics.
4. Tuberoinfundibular: Dopaminergic projections here tonically **inhibit prolactin release**. This is why dopamine is the prolactin inhibiting factor . The projection arise form the hypothalamus to the infundibular region
5. Medullary-periventricular: Neurons in the motor neurons of the vagus. May be involved in eating behaviour.
6. Incertohypothalamic: It appears to regulate the anticipatory motivational phase of copulatory behavior in rats
Newer theories suggest a combination of hyper and hypo- dopaminergic activity. High activity in the mesolimbic pathway and low activity in the mesocortical pathway.


## 2. Serotonin Hypothesis of Schizophrenia
Excess levels is hypothesized to cause both positive and negative symptoms of Schizophrenia.
5-HT 2A -receptor blockade is a key factor in the mechanism of action of the main class of **atypical antipsychotic drugs**.
The discovery of  5-HT 2A -receptor and 5-HT2C stimulation was the basis for the hallucinatory effects of hallucinogens like LSD(Lysergic acid diethylamide)
