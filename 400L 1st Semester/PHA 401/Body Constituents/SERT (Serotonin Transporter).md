The serotonin transporter (SERT) is a glycoprotein with 12 transmembrane regions embedded in the axon  and cell  membranes of serotonergic neurons.

When extracellular serotonin binds to receptors on the transporter, conformational changes occur in the transporter and serotonin, Na+ , and Cl- are moved into the cell.

Binding of intracellular K + then resets the transporter to its original conformation and releases serotonin inside the cell.
