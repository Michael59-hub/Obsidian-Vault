### **Drug class**

- **Typical (first-generation) antipsychotic**
    
- **High-potency butyrophenone derivative**
    

---

## **Mechanism of action**

- **Potent dopamine D₂ receptor antagonist**
    
    - Blocks D₂ receptors in:
        
        - **Mesolimbic pathway** → ↓ positive symptoms of psychosis
            
        - **Nigrostriatal pathway** → extrapyramidal side effects (EPS)
            
        - **Tuberoinfundibular pathway** → ↑ prolactin
            
- Weak antagonism at:
    
    - α₁-adrenergic receptors
        
    - Muscarinic (M₁) receptors (minimal)
        
    - H₁ receptors (minimal)
        

---

## **Pharmacological effects**

### **CNS**

- Antipsychotic effect
    
- Sedation (less than low-potency antipsychotics)
    
- High incidence of **extrapyramidal symptoms**
    
- Can cause **neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS)**
    

### **Endocrine**

- ↑ Prolactin → galactorrhea, gynecomastia, amenorrhea
    

### **Cardiovascular**

- QT prolongation
    
- Orthostatic hypotension (less common)
    

---

## **Pharmacokinetics**

- **Route:** Oral, IM, IV (restricted), depot (haloperidol decanoate)
    
- **Absorption:** Good oral absorption
    
- **Protein binding:** ~90%
    
- **Metabolism:** Hepatic (CYP3A4, CYP2D6)
    
- **Half-life:** ~12–36 hours
    
- **Excretion:** Urine and bile
    

---

## **Therapeutic uses**

- **Schizophrenia** (especially acute psychosis)
    
- **Acute mania**
    
- **Agitation and delirium**
    
- **Tourette’s syndrome**
    
- **Huntington’s chorea**
    
- **Severe behavioral disorders**
    
- **Antiemetic** (rare use)
    

---

## **Adverse effects**

### **Extrapyramidal symptoms (very common)**

- Acute dystonia
    
- Parkinsonism
    
- Akathisia
    
- Tardive dyskinesia (long-term)
    

### **Serious**

- **Neuroleptic malignant syndrome**
    
    - Hyperthermia, rigidity, autonomic instability, ↑ CK
        
- QT prolongation → torsades de pointes
    

### **Other**

- Hyperprolactinemia
    
- Sedation
    
- Weight gain (less than atypicals)
    
- Sexual dysfunction
    

---

## **Contraindications**

- Parkinson’s disease
    
- Lewy body dementia
    
- Severe CNS depression
    
- History of NMS
    
- Prolonged QT interval
    

---

## **Drug interactions**

- ↑ QT prolongation with other QT-prolonging drugs
    
- CNS depressants → ↑ sedation
    
- CYP inhibitors → ↑ haloperidol levels
    
- Levodopa → antagonized
    

---

## **Special features / points to remember**

- **High potency → more EPS, less sedation**
    
- Minimal anticholinergic effects
    
- No effect on negative symptoms compared to atypicals
    
- Available as **long-acting depot preparation**
